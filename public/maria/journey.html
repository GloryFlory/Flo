I
Chapter One

Kuala
Lumpur

Three days to find each other again

After months apart, every sense comes alive at once — the humidity on your skin, the scent of street food, and her. The city becomes a backdrop for rediscovery.

🤗
The 33-Hour Hug

World record attempt or not — hold each other at the airport and don't let go. Let time dissolve. No words needed yet.

🌃
City Wander

Get lost in KL's layered streets. Chinatown lanterns, street art, rooftop bars above the chaos. Hands intertwined the whole time.

🍜
Eat Everything

Hawker stalls, roti canai at 2am, laksa on plastic stools. Feed each other bites, argue about favourites. Let the food be intimacy.

🛁
Slow Mornings

Nowhere to be. Coffee in bed, sunlight through curtains, stories from the months apart. Let the pace be dictated by nothing but desire.

✦ ✦ ✦
II
Chapter Two

Bali

Seven days of depth, delight, and devotion

The island holds its own magic — rice terraces at dawn, incense smoke, the sound of the ocean never far away. Here you go deeper.

The Blindfold Day

A full day surrendered to the other senses. Sight removed, everything else sharpens — touch, sound, scent, taste, presence. A tantric journey built in layers, each one asking more trust than the last.

I

Opening — Breathwork Together

Begin seated, facing each other, blindfolds on. A shared breathwork practice to arrive — connected breathing, synchronising rhythms, dissolving the busyness of the mind. Let this be the threshold.

II

Trust Walk — Leading Through Space

One leads, one follows. Move through your space together — hands guiding, no words. Then swap. Feel how differently you navigate the world when you cannot see. This is the beginning of profound trust.

III

Free Wander — Solitary Drift

Each of you wanders alone in the same space, blindfolded. Feel the edges, the textures, the air. Occasionally you'll find each other — fingertips brushing, a quiet collision, a moment of recognition.

IV

Blindfold Dance — Moving Without Watching

Music on. Let your bodies move together without choreography, without self-consciousness. No one is watching. Feel where the music takes you, feel where she takes you.

V

Sensory Feast — Feeding Each Other

A spread of fruits, textures, flavours — mango, salt, honey, something unexpected. Take turns feeding each other, slowly. Each bite is offered with intention. Taste becomes the whole world.

VI

Stillness — Time Apart, Together

Each of you retreats into quiet. Meditation, journalling, simply lying down. The same space, two inner worlds. No agenda. Let whatever is present, be present.

VII

Closing — Into Each Other

The day has been building toward this. Fully present. Fully awake in the body. Blindfolds finally lifted or kept on — your choice. Explore each other with the devotion the day has cultivated.

The Playful Day

Because love that can't laugh together is only half-love.

🏖️

Dig a Hole

Find a beach. Dig an absolutely pointless, ambitious hole. Bury each other up to the neck. Take photos. Be children.

🤸

Acro Together

Your shared language. Build flows on the beach or in a park. Bird, stardust, whatever comes. The trust is already there.

💃

Spontaneous Dance

Put on a playlist and dance wherever you are — a market, a rice field, your kitchen. No stage needed.

🌊

Ocean Play

Waves, jumping, body surfing, pretending to be sea creatures. Laugh until your stomach hurts.

🎭

Invent a Game

Make up the rules as you go. Some kind of chase, a challenge, a dare. Whatever emerges from the mood.

🌅

Sunset Ritual

End each Bali day watching the sun go down. Pick a new spot each time. Mark the moment together.

🧘
Yoga for Two

Partner yoga at sunrise. Ubud's morning air, a shared mat, the kind of practice that becomes conversation.

🌿
Rice Terrace Mornings

Tegallalang at first light before the crowds. Walk the paths. Let the green silence do its work.

🛕
Temple Offerings

Tanah Lot at dusk. Uluwatu at sunset. Place an offering together. Bali asks for reverence — give it.

🔥
Evening Fires

A warung, cold Bintang, candle between you. Conversations that stretch past midnight. The ones that matter.

Maybe Chapter Three

Sumba

Wild, unhurried, unknown

If Bali is magic, Sumba is myth. Rugged coastlines, megalithic villages, horses on open savannah, waves that feel discovered for the first time. Come here when you want the world to shrink to just the two of you and a horizon.

No schedule. No itinerary. Just arrive and see what the island offers.

What you carry into it

  • The ease that comes after weeks together
  • Bodies that remember each other fully
  • The conversations that only come with time
  • An appetite for rawness and beauty
  • Nothing to prove, nowhere else to be

"After months of distance, what you're really planning isn't a trip —
it's a homecoming."
